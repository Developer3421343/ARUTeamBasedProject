Probability

Probability is the branch of mathematics that deals with measuring and analyzing uncertainty. It provides a framework for quantifying how likely an event is to occur, expressed as a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain). For example, flipping a fair coin gives a probability of 0.5 for landing heads and 0.5 for tails. Probability is widely applied in everyday life whether predicting weather, assessing risks in business, or modeling outcomes in science and engineering. At its core, it helps us make informed decisions when outcomes are not guaranteed.

1. A bag contains 3 red, 2 blue, and 5 green balls. What is the probability of picking a blue ball?

2. A fair coin is flipped twice. What is the probability of getting two heads?

3. A dice is rolled. What is the probability of getting an even number?


Probability is a powerful tool that helps us understand and manage uncertainty in everyday life. By comparing favorable outcomes to all possible outcomes, it allows us to measure chances, predict results, and make better decisions in situations where certainty is not guaranteed.

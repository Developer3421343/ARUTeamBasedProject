Pythagoras' Theorem

Definition

Pythagoras' Theorem states that for any right-angled triangle, the square of the longest side (hypotenuse) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.
The formula is: a2 + b2 = c2
a and b are the shorter sides and c is the the longest side (hypotenuse).

Pythagorean Theorem is used to show that a triangle is a right angled. Or to find the length of one side when the other two are known.
For Example, 32 + 42 = 9 + 16 = 25 = 52

What if it's 0? If 1 is the exponent then 91 = 9 and if the exponent is 0 then 90 = 1, 00 = 1

Check your understanding

  • Hypotenuse is the longest side of a right-angled triangle.
  • The formula is a2 + b2 = c2.
  • Pythagorean Triple is when all 3 sides are integers
    For example, 3, 4 and 5

How to calculate the hypotenuse using Pythagoras' Theorem:

  1. Label the sides of the triangle a, b and c
    Hypotenuse which is the opposite the right-angle, should always be labelled 𝑐.
  2. Substitute the values into the formula a² + b² = c²
  3. Calculate a² + b², then square root c²

Structure:

In the first part of this lesson, We will explore calculation structure and how to use it. You will be introduced to BIDMAS and BODMAS.

  • BIDMAS / BODMAS
    BIDMAS - Brackets, Indices, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction
    BODMAS - Brackets, Orders, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction
    Both BIDMAS and BODMAS are a set of rules that are used to decide in which order you solve parts of a math equation or expression. Though the wording may look confusing, although they are both the same thing, just worded differently!

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks
    Both BIDMAS and BODMAS prevent people from reaching different answers to the same calculation. By using BID/BOD we can ensure everyone calculates using the same method, therefore theoretically arriving at the same answer. Without a rule like this we would end up with three people potentially arriving at three seperate answers from the same question! It is especially important in STEM fields ensuring critical calculations are done correctly, if multiple people are working on a task you want to be sure they are all using the same form of mathematics!

Types of Calculation

In this part of the lesson we will be exploring some different types of more advanced calculation

Measures:

Types of

  • Standard Form

    - Standard form is a way of writing very large or very small numbers using powers of 10.
    It is written as: 𝑎 × 10𝑛

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks
    Finding the standard form makes smaller or larger numbers much easier to read, write, and calculate with. Its used most commonly in STEM fields as it allows a huge range of numbers to be used much more efficiently, both small and large.



  • Prime Factors

    - Prime factors are the prime numbers that can be multiplied together to make another number, a prime number can only be divided by 1 and itself.

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks Prime factors might seem very theoretical, but they’re useful because they help break numbers into their basic building blocks, which makes many everyday calculations easier and more efficient.



  • Trigonometry

    - Trigonometry can be described as the relationship between angles and sides of triangles, usually right handed triangles! It uses special ratios to calculate the unknown sides and/or angles.

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks
    Trigonometry is especially useful when an object may be too large, or too unsafe even to measure conventionally, for example a bulding. Surveyors can use the SOH CAH TOA ratios to find out the dimensions Without physically going up and measuring. It can even help with designing a staircase, or a ramp for accessible access.

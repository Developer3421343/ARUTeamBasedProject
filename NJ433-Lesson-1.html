Prime Numbers

Definition

A Prime Number is a whole number greater than 1, that has exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

For Example 11 is only divisible by 1 and 11. It also needs to be a positive number. The opposite of a prime number is a composite number (more than 2 factors).

A Composite Number has more than 2 factors.

Key points to remember 🎯

  • It must be a whole number
  • Must be greater than 1
  • Exactly two distinct divisors.

Note: 1 is NOT a prime number. To be a prime number, it should have exactly two factors, 1 only has itself.

❗Common Mistakes

  • 1 Is a Prime Number?
    1 only has one factor, it doesn't have 2 factors
  • All Odd numbers are Prime Numbers?
    Many Odd numbers are composite, like 9, 15, 21
  • 2 Is the Only Even Prime Number?
    2 is the only even number that is prime. Every other even number has more than two factors

Examples

Number Factors Prime? Why
2 1,2 Yes Exactly 2 factors
3 1,3 Yes Exactly 2 factors
4 1,2,4 No More than 2 factors
7 1,7 Yes Exactly 2 factors

Prime Numbers Quiz 🧠

1. Is 53 a prime number?
Answer: Yes

2. Is 223 a prime number?
Answer: Yes

3. List the Prime Numbers between 50 and 60?
Answer: 53, 59

4. Which of the following is a prime number?
13, 15, 21
Answer: 13

5. How many prime numbers are there between 10 and 25?
5, 4, 7
Answer: 5

Circles

Radius & diameter


The diameter of a cirlce is the total width of a circle

The radius is the distance between the center od the circle and any one point on the edge

The radius is always half of the diameter

diameter = radius × 2

likewise

radius = diameter ÷ 2





Circumference


The circumference of a circle is the total length of the distance around the circle

The circumference of a circle can be calculated by multiplying the diameter by pi (π)

circumference = π × diameter

An easy way of measuring the circumference of real, physical circles is to run a piece of string around the circumference then measure the length with a ruler

Area


The area of a circle can be calculated by multiplying pi by the radius squared

area = π × radius²

More parts


A chord is a line joining any 2 points on the circumference of a circle

An arc is a section of the circumference between two given points

A segment is the area enclosed between an arc and it's chord

A sector is the area enclose between two radii and their arc

A tangent is a straight line which touches only one point on the edge of a circle and is perpendicular to the radius of that point

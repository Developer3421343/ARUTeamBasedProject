Perimeter and Area/Surface Area

  • Perimeter - The perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a 2D shape. You find it by adding up the lengths of all the sides. For example, to find the perimeter of a rectangle, you add the lengths of all four sides together. Perimeter is measured in units of length, like centimeters, meters, or inches.

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks
    Finding the perimeter is useful in everyday tasks as it allows us to ascertain the correct amount of materials to buy for a task like fencing off a garden. Perimeter calculations are most used in construction when precise measurements are required to ensure that spaces are correctly sized, and properly enclosed. Perimeter calculations can also be used to figure out how large the outlines of different shapes, are even if their areas are different.



  • Area - Area is the amount of space inside a 2D shape. It tells you how much surface is covered within the boundaries of the shape. For example, the area of a rectangle is found by multiplying its length by its width. Area is measured in square units, such as square centimeters (cm²) or square meters (m²).

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks Working out the area of soemthing can tell you how much space is actually inside a 2D space, extremely useful for working out whether or not you can fit a certain item of furniture into a room for example! It allows you also to compare how much difference there is in space between two areas, even if they are a completely different shape. It is often used again in construction for ascertaining how much material is needed to cover a surface; think roof tiles, wallpaper/paint, grass for a lawn, the list goes on!



  • Surface Area - Surface area is the total area of all the outer faces of a 3D object. It shows how much space covers the outside of a solid shape, like a cube or a cylinder. To find surface area, you calculate the area of each face and add them together. Surface area is also measured in square units, like cm² or m².

    Why it is useful in everyday tasks The surface area allows us to see how large the oputer surfaces of any given 3d shape are to give an example, this is extremely useful when figuring out how much of a material you need to use to wrap something. It also proves particularly important in the realm of science when working out how qucikly things will heat, cool, or react depending on the surface area exposed. Engineers and designers use surface area to work out material costs, efficiency, and how many things will fucntion in real life scenarios!

Practice Questions (With Workings)


Perimeter

  • Question One: Rectangle

    A rectangle has a length of 12 cm and a width of 5 cm; find the perimeter.

    Formula

    Perimeter = 2 × (length + width)

    Working:

    P = 2 × (12 + 5)
    P = 2 × 17
    P = 34 cm



  • Question Two: Triangle

    A triangle has sides 7 cm, 9 cm, and 11 cm. Find the perimeter.

    Formula

    Perimeter = side + side + side

    Working:

    P = 7 + 9 + 11
    P = 27 cm



  • Question Three: Other Shapes - Pentagon, Hexagon etc

    Each side of a regular pentagon is 6 cm. Find the perimeter.

    Formula

    Perimeter = number of sides × side length

    Working:

    P = 5 × 6
    P = 30 cm

    A rectangle has a length of 8 m and width of 3 m. Find the area.

    Formula

    Area = length × width

    Working:

    A = 8 x 3
    A = 24 m²

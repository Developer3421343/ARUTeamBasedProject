Ratio and Fraction

A fraction is a way of representing a part of a whole. It is written as two numbers separated by a line: the top number (numerator) shows how many parts we have, and the bottom number (denominator) shows how many equal parts the whole is divided into. For example, 34 means three out of four equal parts.

A ratio, on the other hand, compares two quantities directly. It shows how many times one value contains another or how they relate in size. Ratios are often written with a colon, like 3:4, or as a fraction. For instance, if there are 3 apples and 4 oranges, the ratio of apples to oranges is 3:4.

1. In a class, the ratio of boys to girls is 3:5. If there are 40 students, how many are girls?

2. Simplify the ratio 12:18

3. A recipe needs 3/4 cup of sugar. How much sugar is needed for double the recipe?


Fractions and ratios both describe relationships between numbers, but in different ways. A fraction expresses a part of a whole, showing how many equal parts are taken out of the total, while a ratio compares two separate quantities directly to highlight their relative sizes. Together, they provide simple yet powerful tools for understanding proportions, whether we are dividing something into parts or comparing one group to another.
